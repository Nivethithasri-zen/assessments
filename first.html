History of marvel

Marvel is a renowned entertainment company known for its extensive universe of comic books, movies, and TV shows. In 2008, Marvel Studios embarked on an ambitious journey by releasing "Iron Man," which laid the foundation for the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU). This interconnected universe revolutionized the superhero genre and captivated audiences worldwide. Throughout the 2000s, Marvel Comics experienced significant success with critically acclaimed storylines such as "Civil War" and "Secret Invasion." The comics introduced iconic characters like Iron Man, Captain America, Thor, and the Hulk, capturing the imaginations of readers. In 2012, Marvel achieved an extraordinary milestone with "The Avengers," bringing together various superheroes in a single film. This groundbreaking achievement set the stage for an unprecedented expansion of the MCU, with subsequent films and TV series weaving intricate narratives and introducing new characters.
